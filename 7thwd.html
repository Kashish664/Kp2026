Dr. Norman Borlaug

The man who saved a billion lives

A Summary of His Work

Dr. Norman Borlaug (1914-2009) was an American agronomist who led initiatives worldwide that contributed to the extensive increases in agricultural production termed the Green Revolution. He was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1970 in recognition of his contributions to world peace through increasing food supply. Borlaug is often credited with saving over a billion people worldwide from starvation.

Key Achievements

  • **1940s-1950s:** Developed semi-dwarf, high-yield, disease-resistant wheat varieties in Mexico.
  • **1960s:** Introduced his varieties to Pakistan and India, significantly increasing their wheat yields and preventing widespread famine.
  • **1970:** Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for his work.
  • **Global Impact:** His methods and varieties were adopted globally, drastically reducing food insecurity in many nations.
